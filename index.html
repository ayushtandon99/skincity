Arishta / Asava (Fermented Formulation)

History

Arishta and Asava are classical Ayurvedic formulations with roots in ancient texts like the Charaka Samhita. These preparations have been used for centuries, showcasing the deep understanding of fermentation processes in Ayurveda.

Application

Arishta and Asava are herbal medicines made through natural fermentation. Arishta is prepared by fermenting herbal decoctions, while Asava uses freshly extracted herbal juices. Both formulations are potent for digestion, immunity boosting, and addressing conditions like respiratory disorders, digestive issues, and general debility. The fermentation process enhances bioavailability, making these products fast-acting and effective.

Bhasma (Calcinated Drug)

History

Bhasma has a long history in Ayurveda, dating back to ancient texts that document its preparation through a rigorous purification process called Shodhana. This method emphasizes the importance of transforming metals and minerals into bioavailable forms.

Application

Bhasma refers to fine ash obtained by calcining metals, minerals, or herbs. It is used to treat chronic diseases due to its high potency and easy absorption. Bhasma formulations are especially effective for conditions such as anemia, digestive disorders, and enhancing mental clarity. Common types include Swarn Bhasma (gold) and Yashad Bhasma (zinc), known for boosting immunity and supporting bone health.
